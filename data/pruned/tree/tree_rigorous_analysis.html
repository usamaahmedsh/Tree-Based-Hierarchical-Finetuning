Hierarchical Stratification Tree Analysis

Context & Methodology

This report rigidly analyzes the output of Stage 1: Hierarchical Stratification Tree as defined by the provided de-biasing methodology. The objective of the tree is to partition the corpus into intersectional subgroups (leaves) and assign a mathematically derived Majority Score s(l) to every leaf.

This score identifies structurally overrepresented nodes (Majority Groups) that will later receive HIGH or MEDIUM intensity asymmetric noise during Stage 3 to blur their dominance, while preserving the signal of underrepresented LOW nodes.

1. Structural Imbalance & Noise Targeting

The core thesis of the paper relies on the fact that raw Wikipedia text contains highly imbalanced "Majority" nodes. The charts below validate this.

Analysis: The histogram (Log Scale) shows severe positive skew. Thousands of leaves contain only 1-5 chunks, representing weak signals, while a very small handful of leaves contain hundreds of chunks.

Analysis: Mapping the tree data directly to the Stage 3 parameters: 42247 chunks sit in heavily saturated nodes and will be targeted for harsh asymmetric noise (Entity Replacement, Topic Injection). Conversely, the majority of the minority signal (100583 docs) will be shielded.

Analysis: The document count maps entirely linearly to the calculated Majority Score. This directly confirms the integrity of the math `(count - mu) / sigma` applied to the nodes. Only the dots drifting entirely into the top-right quadrant trigger the 1.5 score threshold.
